About Me

I am a Technical Game Designer with a strong technical background stemming from a Master's Degree in Computer Science and professional training at Digital Bros Game Academy. I have a fascination with games that achieve depth through simplicity and thematic cohesion. My experience spans diverse genres, including action RPGs, shooters, deckbuilders, and racing games. Additionally, I love exploring simple yet clever indie titles such as Mewgenics, Inscryption, Outer Wilds, and Superliminal.
Naturally open-minded, focused, and always eager to learn.

AnotheReality · Technical Game Designer · 2019–2020 · Unity
CollaborativeMaze
Overview

Final thesis project developed in collaboration with startup AnotheReality. I designed a VR collaborative game for corporate team-building activities and built a tool that procedurally generates game instances of desired difficulty. Difficulty is measured by AI agents with different human-like behaviours (prudent, aggressive, smart) that simulate gameplay and produce intensity diagrams. Published in IEEE scientific journal.

My Contributions
  • Designed the VR collaborative game experience and its team-building mechanics
  • Developed the procedural content generation tool for difficulty-controlled maze instances
  • Implemented AI agents with human-like behaviour profiles to simulate and measure difficulty
  • Wrote an extensive thesis on PCG history, usages in media, and maze/map generation algorithms
  • Research published in IEEE scientific journal

Technical Game Designer · 2020 · Unreal Engine 4 · Python / TensorFlow
Neuroevolution Driving
Overview

A personal project combining Unreal Engine 4 and Neural Networks. Starting from UE4's VehicleAdvanced template and a TensorFlow plugin, I trained a neural network with neuroevolutionary techniques to drive a vehicle around a full track without crashing. Agents trained on complex tracks generalised correctly to unseen irregular tracks.

Technical Details
  • UE4 VehicleAdvanced template as base environment
  • TensorFlow (Python) integrated via UE4 plugin
  • Neuroevolutionary training: population selection, mutation and fitness evaluation
  • Tested best models on unseen irregular tracks to validate generalisation
  • Compared models trained on simple vs complex tracks to analyse overfitting

Technical Game Designer · 2020 · Unreal Engine 4 · Behaviour Trees / EQS
DailyLife Simulator
Overview

A personal project to master Unreal Engine's Behaviour Trees and Environment Query System (EQS). NPCs navigate a house and perform a realistic daily routine autonomously, choosing from a variety of solo and cooperative activities, then going to sleep at night.

Technical Details
  • Behaviour Trees driving all NPC decision-making and activity scheduling
  • Environment Query System (EQS) for spatial awareness and activity location selection
  • Solo activities: Idle, Sitting, Reading, Smoking, Drinking Coffee, Playing on a console
  • Cooperative activities: Conversation, Ping Pong, Playing on a console together
  • Night cycle: NPCs stop all activities and autonomously go to sleep
